# Project Axiom

## Design Philosophy

This is not a portfolio.

This is a carefully crafted digital experience that represents the way I think, solve problems, and engineer products.

The goal is not to impress people with animations.

The goal is to make people remember the person behind the work.

---

# Brand Statement

Engineering deserves elegance.

Technology should feel calm.

Interfaces should disappear.

Experiences should remain.

---

# Visitor Journey

The experience should create the following emotions:

Curiosity

↓

Interest

↓

Respect

↓

Trust

↓

"I want this person on my team."

---

# Personality

The website should feel

- Curious
- Intelligent
- Calm
- Confident
- Premium
- Intentional
- Editorial
- Timeless
- Crafted

Never

- Loud
- Flashy
- Futuristic
- Gaming
- Cyberpunk
- AI generated
- Dashboard
- Template

---

# Visual Inspirations

Apple

Leica

Porsche

Linear

Aesop

Awwwards (selected editorial websites)

The goal is not to copy these brands.

The goal is to understand why they feel premium.

---

# Design Principles

## Less but Better

Every element must justify its existence.

---

## Typography is the Hero

Typography carries emotion.

Not color.

Not decoration.

---

## Whitespace Creates Luxury

Empty space is not empty.

It allows the content to breathe.

---

## Motion Has Purpose

Animation should guide attention.

Never distract from content.

---

## Precision Over Decoration

Every component should feel engineered.

Not assembled.

---

## Calm Interfaces

The interface should never compete with the content.

The UI disappears.

The story remains.

---

# Navigation Philosophy

Navigation is not a menu.

Navigation is a physical object.

Inspired by

- Leica camera controls
- Porsche interior switches
- Editorial indexes
- Premium notebooks

It should feel tactile.

Confident.

Minimal.

---

# Hero Philosophy

The hero should create curiosity.

Not explain everything.

The visitor should immediately feel

"This person is different."

The hero is allowed to be quiet.

Confidence does not need to shout.

---

# Color Philosophy

Do not think in colors.

Think in materials.

- Charcoal
- Soft graphite
- Warm paper
- Brushed metal
- Frosted glass
- Warm white

Avoid bright saturated colors.

Avoid trendy gradients.

---

# Motion Philosophy

Motion should be invisible.

The best animation is the one people feel rather than notice.

Timing should be slow.

Movement should feel natural.

---

# Layout Philosophy

Design pages like editorial spreads.

Not dashboards.

Every section should feel like a chapter.

Every scroll should reveal the next scene.

---

# Component Philosophy

Every component should feel reusable.

Every spacing value should be intentional.

Every radius should belong to the same system.

Nothing should feel random.

---

# Success Criteria

When someone closes the website, they should remember:

"This person has exceptional taste."

"This person cares about details."

"I want this person on my team."

Not

"Nice animations."

Not

"Cool portfolio."

The experience should be remembered more than the interface.
