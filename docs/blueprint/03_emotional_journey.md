# Project Axiom — Emotional Journey (03_emotional_journey.md)

This document maps the user's emotional progression through the digital experience, outlining the triggers and transitions that convert a casual visitor into a trusting collaborator.

---

## The Emotional Map

```
  [ Landing ]
       ↓ (Trigger: Disorientation & Broken Scanning Habit)
  [ Curiosity ]
       ↓ (Trigger: Rhythmic Stagger & Mindset Disclosures)
  [ Discovery ]
       ↓ (Trigger: Radical Candor & Documented Failures)
  [ Trust ]
       ↓ (Trigger: Intellectual Affinity & Open Door)
  [ Conversation ]
```

---

## 1. Landing (State: Disorientation → Disruption)

* **Sensory Triggers**: A warm, near-black space (`#0f0f0f`). A minimal `+` crosshair instead of a pointer. Staggered, massive cream text sliding up in complete silence. No images. No logo clouds.
* **Visitor’s Internal Monologue**: 
  > *"Where is the developer? Where is the visual noise? Why is this site not begging for my attention?"*
* **Why the transition exists**: Juries and tech leaders scan portfolios on autopilot. By removing the expected visual markers (portraits, hero illustrations, dashboard structures), we break their automated scanning habit. They must orient themselves. To orient, they must read.

---

## 2. Curiosity (State: Intrigue → Engagement)

* **Sensory Triggers**: The core thesis (*"Systems that endure are built by people who understand them first"*). Transitioning into Chapter 02 (Perspective) via smooth, controlled scroll. The layout staggers left and right, slowing the speed of reading.
* **Visitor’s Internal Monologue**: 
  > *"This person isn't listing their skills. They are explaining how they evaluate problems. 'Cognitive load is technical debt.' That's true. Let's see if they can back this up."*
* **Why the transition exists**: Curiosity is born when there is a gap between a compelling claim and the evidence for it. The reader is intrigued by the principles but remains skeptical. They scroll down looking for proof.

---

## 3. Discovery (State: Verification → Realization)

* **Sensory Triggers**: Chapter 03 (Case Files). The format shifts to a highly structured investigative record. Wide margins, monospaced labels, direct technical language.
* **Visitor’s Internal Monologue**: 
  > *"This is a real architecture. They ran into request loop bottlenecks during payroll runs. They evaluated event sourcing, rejected it, and chose job queues instead. They know exactly why Kong or Lua wouldn't work for their team."*
* **Why the transition exists**: Technical decision-makers verify credentials by observing how a developer navigates ambiguity. By laying out the constraints and options explored, we invite the visitor into the developer's raw thought process.

---

## 4. Trust (State: Safety → Intellectual Affinity)

* **Sensory Triggers**: Reading the **Reflection** field of the Case Files (e.g., *"Observability was added reactively, not proactively... A silent failure surfaced in a payroll run review two weeks later"*). Continuing into the Expedition chapter where the developer's growth is framed through mistakes and insights.
* **Visitor’s Internal Monologue**: 
  > *"Every portfolio tells me the builder is a genius who never makes mistakes. This engineer tells me exactly what they got wrong, what they'd change, and what remains unresolved. They have engineering integrity."*
* **Why the transition exists**: **Trust is built on vulnerability, not perfection.** By documenting failures and limitations openly, we establish undeniable credibility. Juries and CTOs know that every system has flaws. Denying them destroys trust; documenting them builds it.

---

## 5. Conversation (State: Action → Intent)

* **Sensory Triggers**: The Toolkit chapter demonstrating curated instruments, leading into the Connect screen. The final statement: *"The most interesting conversations usually begin with a difficult problem."*
* **Visitor’s Internal Monologue**: 
  > *"I don't need to interview this person to see if they fit a keyword list. I have a scaling bottleneck in our queue system right now, and I want this specific mind looking at it. I will send them an email."*
* **Why the transition exists**: The transition to contact is not framed as a sales pitch ("Hire me"). It is framed as an intellectual invitation to solve a puzzle. The visitor does not feel like they are reaching out to a job candidate; they feel like they are consulting an expert.

---

## Creative Director Questions for the Builder

Before we move to `04_experience.md`, review these emotional checkpoints:

1. **Are we hitting the "Vulnerability" note too quietly?** The Reflections in Chapter 03 contain our most potent trust-building tool. Should they have slightly more visual prominence, or does keeping them in the same muted styling preserve the "journal" feel?
2. **How does the cursor hover influence the emotional state?** Currently, hovering over interactive elements changes the cursor from a crosshair to an expanded shape with an amber pip. Does this feel like an engineered confirmation of target focus (building confidence), or is it too playful?
3. **Is the transition from Trust to Conversation earned?** In standard web design, there is a giant CTA button at the end. Here, there is only a line of text and a link. Does this calm end create confidence, or does it risk leaving the visitor hanging?
